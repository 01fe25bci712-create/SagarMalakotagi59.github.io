Lab Program 1

Dataset: Lung Capacity Dataset

Objective

To analyze the Lung Capacity dataset using histograms, boxplots, and descriptive statistics such as mean, median, mode, quartiles and coefficient of variation.

Dataset Description

The dataset contains the following columns:

  • LungCap
  • Age
  • Height
  • Smoke
  • Gender
  • Caesarean

Histogram Analysis

1. Histogram of Age

The histogram shows the distribution of ages in the dataset. Most individuals are concentrated in the lower to mid-age range.

2. Histogram of Lung Capacity

The lung capacity values are mostly concentrated at moderate levels, with fewer individuals having very low or very high lung capacity.

3. Histogram of Height

The most common height range lies around the middle values, indicating an average height for most individuals.

4. Height Distribution by Gender

From the histogram, males are generally taller than females.

Boxplot Analysis

1. Boxplot of Lung Capacity

The boxplot shows the spread and variability of lung capacity values along with median and possible outliers.

2. Lung Capacity: Smokers vs Non-Smokers

Non-smokers generally tend to have slightly higher lung capacity compared to smokers.

3. Height by Gender

The median height of males is greater than that of females.

Descriptive Statistics

Mean

  • Mean Lung Capacity: 7.86
  • Mean Age: 12.33
  • Mean LungCap (Males): 8.31
  • Mean LungCap (Females): 7.41

Median

  • Median Height for Smokers: 69.0
  • Median LungCap (Males): 8.35
  • Median LungCap (Females): 7.75

Mode

Most frequent Caesarean status: No

Quartiles

  • First Quartile (Q1) of LungCap: 6.15
  • Third Quartile (Q3) of LungCap: 9.8

Coefficient of Variation

  • CV (Caesarean = Yes): 33.13%
  • CV (Caesarean = No): 34.09%
  • CV for Age below 12: 36.34%

Conclusion

This lab helped in understanding data visualization and descriptive statistics using real-world health data. Lung capacity is influenced by age, gender, and smoking habits.
